The diagnosis of food allergies was the subject of comparative research conducted in 2013 by Philippe Bégin and Kari C. Nadeau. Research has shown that it may be difficult to interpret allergy test findings and that the patient history is often incorrect when screening for and treating food allergies [7].... Consequently, relying exclusively on medical history to monitor dietary allergens that cause allergies will not be adequate. Additionally, studies have shown that when an allergen enters the stomach, in addition to instantly causing local symptoms (skin responses) upon contact with the meal, it may also result in excruciating stomach pain and prolonged vomiting. Therefore, it is conceivable to infer that methods for recording symptoms may be used to identify potential food allergies. To determine the links between food consumption, mental discomfort, and digestive issues, Egbert Clevers, Hans Törnblom, Magnus Simrén, Jan Tack, and Lukas Van Oudenhove performed another study in 2016.

The major goal of this study was to demonstrate how patterns in food consumption and symptomatology may be utilized to anticipate digestive issues. A variety of challenges relating to the forecasting and assessment of the input data utilizing tools like machine learning have also been addressed in the study. It has been shown that there is a link between food consumption and symptoms, which may be examined using deep learning and machine learning. You could only notice poor weight gain and no discomfort or other symptoms in young children.

Techniques from machine learning were used in this performance. Three classification models were trained independently to determine the diabetes status and offer meal plans for the user.

Research Objectives

The main objective of the proposed research is to make people acquainted with their daily meal habits, food allergies, and calorie intake. ...

  • Elimination of Allergens

    • The system shall predict the food item/allergens cause the allergy by analyzing the entered data for three weeks. It is likely to be affected if frequently consumed food items are within the 'big eight'; the eight food groups responsible for 90% of food allergy reactions. Once the food item is predicted, it can be eliminated. This elimination of Allergens involves removing a particular food from the user's diet for two weeks. That way, the users know that the food eliminated was causing the allergies and can be refrained. The app would aid in removing food allergies with the help of analyzing and predicting techniques.

  • Symptom Tracking

    • Analyze the symptoms entered by the user and predict the allergy the user has concerning the food item or the allergens that caused the allergy. The user can select their prevailing symptoms from a list of medical symptoms provided through the app (medical symptoms will be filtered concerning the food items entered into the system) and record them as part of the prediction. Users can record the details as needed in the symptoms section of check-in for three weeks. The above symptom tracking feature would help predict the user's allergy.

  • Informed decision making

    • The user can become an active participant in any Allergy-healthcare decision, feeling empowered to discuss procedures, self-diagnoses, and treatments with various healthcare specialists as the app provides 95% accurate medical decisions. 

  • Generation of PDF reports

    • The reports may contain information such as the system predicted food item/allergens, symptoms entered by the user, any other relevant symptoms (detected by the application itself), the system predicted allergy, quick remedies to be taken, and outcomes. These reports can help users discover triggers or help them stay on track to minimize symptoms based on allergies & allergens

  • Create a Forum to active discussion

    • The purpose of this platform is to engage people worldwide to ask questions about ask doubts and questions of food allergies and post their recipes that do not contain allergens. This enables sharing knowledge on food allergies and help people to aware of their Food Allergen. And have a voting system to display the reliability of the content.

  • Profile for User

    • There are people with different Food Allergens, and they have their own specific Allergies, so we must customize them for the user. The profile will take user details of food allergy. This help user to identify another user through the forum and it is easy to make a specific suggestion according to user Allergy content.

  • Process Accurate Recipes

    • In our method, a food context and allergy-free substitute ingredients are extracted from known food recipes, and they are attached to unknown food recipes based on the similarity between recipes. In order to calculate the recipe similarity, we extract the following recipe metadata from each recipe. The information of ingredients and equipment is extracted and used as keywords in a recipe. The context metadata is a set of keywords for capturing the food context in the targeted dish,

    such as the function of an allergic component, the texture, and the color, which are not explicitly defined in the recipe. As a result, in this study, a cooking expert extracts context metadata from a small portion of the recipe. To find allergy-free ingredients for a specific recipe, our method first computes the similarity between the targeted recipe and the user’s Input allergen and process using NLP. Then, using our method, we extract allergy-free ingredients with scores, which are then associated with recipes in the top rank. Furthermore, each allergy-free ingredient's total score is computed, and the allergy-free ingredients with the highest total scores are recommended for the targeted food.

  • Food Restriction Filter System

    • The purpose of this component is to avoid the user's food restrictions among the allergy free food recipes. Each recipe is categorized into specific user’s food preferences. The user's preferred food restriction is avoided, and that should be altered with the user’s permitted ingredient according to the Figure 10 religious diary restriction diagram.

  • Measure Calorie Counts Through an Image

    • The proposed system is intended to calculate calories using an image. The calorie counter model will count the calories of the meal plan through an image and confirm the user's total calorie intake after the meal plan is provided according to the schedule (according to the schedule provider step - C). Various image processing and machine learning techniques are used to design the Calorie measurement system. It first discovered an image data set and a calorie data set. It took over 1000 food images of various types of Sri Lankan foods and images from kaggle. And the calorie dataset was obtained from a doctor. A doctor contributes to the creation of that dataset by providing advice and information. in this component it includes 3 steps 1) Camera function development, 2) Object detection and segmentation,3) Send notifications. In the following it explains these steps in more detail.

  • Object Detection and Segmentation

    • This component is very important in this system. And if the researcher can obtain an accurate result from this section, it will aid in the development of this system. It develops two models for object detection and food segmentation in this segment. Detecting instances of visual objects of a specific class (such as humans, animals, or cars) in digital images is a significant computer vision task. In this step, it detects the plate first using an object detection model known as MobileNet. It detects plate objects in order to identify the foods individually. When the user captured the image according to the constraints, it identified the plate size and cropped the image. After cropping the image, the segmentation method is used to identify foods that are similar.

    It trained a model to detect objects using pixel counts in this case. MaskRCNN is used during the segmentation process. MaskRCNN is a simple, adaptable, and widely used framework for object instance detection [16]. It then calculates the pixel count of that food object's area. Calorie counts per pixel were saved in the database, and after obtaining the pixel count of foods via MaskRCNN, it manipulates the pixel counts stored in the database, which is calories per pixel count. Finally, this calculation yields the number of calories consumed by the user. Calorie Count = Per pixel calorie count * pixel count of food area.

  • Send Notification

    • Finally, when the calculations and measurements are completed, it checks and sends a notification to the user that the total calorie count has been completed. Following that, if the assigned calorie counts are correctly taken, a notification is sent to the user stating that the user takes this total calorie count. This is accomplished by sending notifications via the Android application.
